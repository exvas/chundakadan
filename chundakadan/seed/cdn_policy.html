COMPANY & HR POLICY

1. Introduction & Governance

1.1 Purpose

  • Establish clear and structured policies for HR, Administration, and Operations
  • Ensure discipline, transparency, and operational efficiency
  • Define roles, responsibilities, and accountability across all levels
  • Create a fair and performance-driven work environment

1.2 Scope

  • Applicable to all employees, interns, consultants, and contract staff
  • Covers all departments, branches, and business operations
  • Includes HR, Admin, Finance, and Operations functions

1.3 Core Values

  • Integrity & Accountability — Honest and responsible actions
  • Professionalism & Respect — Ethical and respectful workplace behavior
  • Performance & Responsibility — Ownership of tasks and results
  • Business & Customer Focus — Commitment to growth and customer satisfaction

1.4 Policy Ownership

  • HR Department — Employee lifecycle, conduct, performance
  • Administration — Facilities, assets, vendor management
  • Operations — Business execution and coordination
  • Management — Final authority and decision-making

1.5 Policy Review

  • Policies will be reviewed periodically or annually
  • Updates may be made based on business requirements
  • Management holds full rights to amend policies

2. Authority & Approval Matrix

2.1 Approval Structure

  • HR — Hiring, onboarding, leave, discipline
  • Admin — Assets, infrastructure, vendors
  • Finance — Expenses, reimbursements, payroll, budgets
  • Management — Final approvals, exceptions, escalations

2.2 Decision Control

  • Employees must act within their role and authority
  • No independent decisions beyond assigned responsibility
  • All approvals must follow the defined reporting hierarchy

3. Employment Framework

3.1 Employment Categories

  • Full-time employees
  • Contract employees
  • Interns
  • Consultants

3.2 Internship Policy

  • Duration: 1 to 6 months
  • Selection based on business requirements
  • Must follow all company policies
  • Performance evaluation is mandatory
  • Certificate issued upon completion
  • Absorption based on performance and vacancy

3.3 Equal Opportunity & Inclusion

  • No discrimination based on gender, caste, religion, or background
  • Equal opportunity for hiring, growth, and development
  • Inclusive and respectful workplace culture

3.4 Code of Conduct & Ethics

  • Maintain discipline, honesty, and professionalism
  • Follow reporting structure and company policies
  • Protect company reputation and interests

3.5 Conflict of Interest

  • Avoid activities conflicting with company interests
  • Mandatory disclosure of any potential conflict
  • Management decision will be final in such cases

4. Talent Acquisition & Onboarding

4.1 Manpower Planning

  • Hiring based on approved business requirements
  • Workforce planning aligned with company goals

4.2 Recruitment Process

  • Approval from management before hiring
  • HR to manage sourcing and coordination

4.3 Selection Process

  • Screening → Interview → Evaluation → Final Approval
  • Selection based on skills, attitude, and role fit

4.4 Offer & Pre-Joining

  • Offer letter issuance
  • Document verification before joining

4.5 Onboarding & Induction

  • Submission of required documents
  • Signing employment agreement
  • Introduction to company policies and culture

4.6 Probation & Confirmation

  • Probation: 3–6 months
  • Performance and behavior-based confirmation

5. Workplace Conduct & Discipline

5.1 Behavior Standards

  • Respectful and professional conduct mandatory
  • Maintain workplace discipline and coordination

5.2 Anti-Harassment & POSH

  • Zero tolerance for harassment or misconduct
  • Sexual harassment complaints handled confidentially
  • Strict disciplinary action for violations

5.3 Dress Code

  • Neat and professional appearance required
  • Dress code may vary for field and office roles

5.4 Disciplinary Process

Any misconduct will follow a structured process:

  1. Identification of issue
  2. Internal review / investigation
  3. Issuance of show-cause notice
  4. Employee explanation within defined timeline
  5. Final decision by management

5.5 Types of Misconduct

  • Fraud, theft, or misappropriation of funds
  • Data breach or confidentiality violation
  • Insubordination or non-compliance
  • Repeated absenteeism or negligence

5.6 Disciplinary Actions

  • Verbal warning
  • Written warning
  • Suspension
  • Termination (based on severity)

6. Work Structure & Attendance

6.1 Working Hours

  • Employees must adhere to assigned schedules
  • Flexibility only with approval

6.2 Attendance & Punctuality

  • Late coming must be reported with valid reason
  • Repeated lateness may lead to disciplinary action

6.3 Field Work

  • Daily reporting mandatory
  • Movement tracking required
  • Travel must be pre-approved

6.4 Remote / Hybrid Work

  • Allowed only with management approval

6.5 Overtime

  • Only with prior approval
  • Based on business requirements

7. Compensation, Benefits & Expenses

7.1 Salary Structure

  • Fixed salary with applicable allowances
  • Monthly payroll processing
  • Statutory deductions (PF, ESI, etc.)

7.2 Incentives

  • Performance-based incentives
  • Linked to KPIs / KRAs

7.3 Reimbursements

  • Valid bills / documents mandatory
  • Submission within defined timeline
  • False claims will lead to strict action

7.4 Allowances

  • Transportation allowance for eligible roles
  • Primarily for field employees

7.5 Employee Benefits

  • PF / ESI (as applicable)
  • Other benefits as per company policy

7.6 Travel Allowances

  • Daily Allowance (DA)
  • Fuel reimbursement: based on actual / fixed rate
  • Accommodation: based on eligibility

7.7 Travel Expense Submission

  • Bills must be submitted
  • No reimbursement without valid proof

8. Leave Policy

8.1 Types of Leave

  • Casual Leave
  • Sick Leave
  • Earned Leave

8.2 Leave Approval

  • Must be approved by reporting manager
  • Planned leave to be applied in advance

8.3 Emergency Leave

  • Must be informed immediately

8.4 Leave Without Pay

  • Applicable when leave balance is exhausted

8.5 Leave Entitlement

  • Casual Leave: 12 days per year
  • Sick Leave
  • Earned Leave

8.6 Leave Rules

  • Leave must be applied in advance (except emergencies)
  • Unused leave carry-forward — as per management policy
  • Leave encashment — if applicable

8.7 Sick Leave

  • Medical certificate required for leave exceeding 2–3 days

9. Performance Management

9.1 Performance Framework

  • Based on performance, behavior, and accountability

9.2 KPI / KRA

  • Defined for each role
  • Monitored regularly

9.3 Appraisal

  • Periodic performance reviews

9.4 Feedback System

  • Continuous feedback encouraged

9.5 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

  • For low-performing employees
  • Improvement timeline defined

10. Learning & Development

10.1 Training

  • Role-based training programs

10.2 Skill Development

  • Continuous learning initiatives

10.3 Certifications

  • External training support (with approval)

ADMINISTRATION & OPERATIONS POLICY MANUAL

A1. Introduction & Governance

A1.1 Purpose of the Policy

  • To establish standardized administrative and operational procedures
  • To ensure smooth day-to-day functioning of the organization
  • To maintain accountability, efficiency, and control in operations

A1.2 Scope & Applicability

  • Applicable to all administrative, operations, and support staff
  • Covers all branches, departments, and business units

A1.3 Policy Ownership & Review

  • Admin / Operations department is responsible for implementation
  • Policies will be reviewed periodically
  • Management holds rights to modify policies

A2. Office Infrastructure & Space Management

A2.1 Office Space Allocation

  • Workspace will be assigned based on role and department
  • Unauthorized changes in seating or space usage are not permitted

A2.2 Workspace Maintenance

  • Employees must maintain cleanliness and organization
  • Damage to office property must be reported immediately

A2.3 Meeting Room Usage

  • Meeting rooms must be booked in advance
  • Rooms must be left clean after use

A3. Asset Management Policy

A3.1 Asset Allocation

  • All company assets (laptops, phones, equipment) will be recorded
  • Assets will be issued based on job requirement

A3.2 Asset Responsibility

  • Employees are responsible for proper use and safety of assets
  • Sharing of assets without approval is not allowed

A3.3 Asset Damage or Loss

  • Must be reported immediately
  • Cost may be recovered based on negligence

A3.4 Asset Register Maintenance

  • Admin team must maintain updated asset records
  • Periodic audits will be conducted

A4.3 Travel Documentation

  • Travel details and purpose must be recorded

A4.4 Travel Allowances

  • Daily Allowance (DA)

A4.5 Travel Expense Submission

  • Bills must be submitted

A5.2 Approval Process

A5.3 Non-Eligible Expenses

A5.4 Fraudulent Claims

  • Submission of false claims will lead to disciplinary action
